Diane J. Ivy


I'm a research scientist in the Program of Atmospheres, Oceans and Climate at MIT working with Susan Solomon. My research is focused on understanding changes in the the stratosphere and how they influence tropospheric climate. I use observational data, numerical models, and statistical analysis to identify robust signals from noise.


I finished my PhD in 2012 in the same department at MIT under Ron Prinn with a focus on trace greenhouse gases and inverse modeling.

Research



Radiative and Dynamical Controls on Stratospheric Temperatures

Radiative and dynamical heating rates control stratospheric temperatures. We used a radiative transfer model to characterize the radiative contributions from ozone loss and increasing well-mixed greenhouse gases to polar stratospheric temperature trends. The Antarctic ozone hole is known to cool in austral spring. Our results show that the radiative cooling due to this pronounced ozone loss is stronger than observed, suggesting dynamical compensation by increased wave activity. In comparison, the Arctic spring cooling due to ozone is weak, and the Arctic winter and spring temperature trends have a strong dynamical contribution
